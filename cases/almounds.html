An iOS application that handles and optimizes the day to day operations of an actual almond processing plant in Central California.

The almond processing plant for the Central California Almond Growers Association (CCAGA) spans 200 acres and holds 30 types of almonds represented by 300 growers, yet is organized and represented by paper-grid with handwritten text from employees. This makes the day-to-day operations of the plant very difficult with a lack of organization and efficiency as the paper grid, meant to represent the vast plant and its current almond loads, uses random placement that wastes money and resources for each load to be placed. Therefore, the administration at the almond processing plant is not equipped with the tools to handle the various robust tasks that are required of the plant.

They need a centralized and modern system in place to handle these day-to-day operations of the processing plant in an efficient and organized manner.

"Why do the workers need this new system?"

 Currently, workers at the processing plant have no way to efficiently place the almond loads being dropped off and typically choose random placement with the hope that it can be placed there under specific requirements on where it can be placed due to the type of almond and the grower. Therefore, these loads consume a lot of time and money required for employees, the land, and the machines at the plant to place these loads on the grid of land to store until further processing. These workers are not equipped to decide efficient dropoff placement for the almond loads, making it difficult to save time, energy, and resources.

"How was the application designed?"

 In order to make design decisions in the best interest of the user, we spoke to employees at the processing plant and revieved a variety of documents they use to clearly understand the issues they face with the current organizational system of the processign plant. From their rfeedback and our own understanding, we found importance in the following points:
  • Users need specific features to be avialable to them as certain types of employees use certain features more or not at all (and shouldn't have the privilege to).
  • Users need flexibility regarding administration privileges to override necessary changes of employees and the manipulation of the grid itself (add, deleting, updating stockpile locations).
  • Users need to be able to enter information about the almond loads type and grower to be used accordingly to determine where the load should be dropped off on the plant.
  • Users need the ability to edit any previous information about loads, grids, or users in an efficient manner.
