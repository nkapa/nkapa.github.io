An iOS application that handles and optimizes the day to day operations of an actual almond processing plant in Central California.

The almond processing plant for the Central California Almond Growers Association (CCAGA) spans 200 acres and holds 30 types of almonds represented by 300 growers, yet is organized and represented by paper-grid with handwritten text from employees. This makes the day-to-day operations of the plant very difficult with a lack of organization and efficiency as the paper grid, meant to represent the vast plant and its current almond loads, uses random placement that wastes money and resources for each load to be placed. Therefore, the administration at the almond processing plant is not equipped with the tools to handle the various robust tasks that are required of the plant.

They need a centralized and modern system in place to handle these day-to-day operations of the processing plant in an efficient and organized manner.
    Compelling Story
  • Workers at the processing plant have no way to efficiently place the almond loads being dropped off
  • Load placement written on paper in a shack on the property
  • Workers at the almond processing plant are not equipped to decide efficient dropoff placement for the almond loads
    Design Decisions
  • 3 types of user interface based on worker status
  • Flow + structure of application based on workload process
  • Flexibility for the administration privileges regarding property + overriding changes
    Features + Talk about What I worked On (Frontend)
  • Administrative, Escort, + Scaler Login (accounts contain user-specific features)
  • Can edit, add, or delete grids, users, loads, and elevators (transports almonds to stockpile)
  • Algorithm used that efficiently orders where almond loads should be dropped off to better manage loads and time
  • Creates assignments for workers to complete + allows employees to override any changes made by the application
  • Administration can easily load and manage employees with access to the application through CSV-importable features
